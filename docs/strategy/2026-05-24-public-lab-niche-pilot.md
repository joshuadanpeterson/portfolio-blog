# Public Automation Lab With Reporter's Notebook

Created: 2026-05-24 23:11 MDT

## Strategy

This site is Josh Peterson's public automation lab: a portfolio, publishing surface, and experiment log for turning messy workflows into repeatable systems. It should not read like a generic programming blog. It should show how journalism, logistics, coding, and AI-assisted operations combine into practical artifacts.

The first niche pilot is small-business AI workflow automation, starting with Google Sheets CRM and follow-up systems for solo consultants, freelancers, and tiny service businesses. The goal is to validate demand through useful artifacts before spinning out a separate brand, newsletter, or niche domain.

## Public Lanes

### Automation Lab

The Automation Lab covers workflow systems, templates, scripts, implementation notes, and case studies. Every major post should ship with at least one practical artifact such as a template, checklist, Apps Script snippet, teardown, or before-and-after workflow.

Initial anchor topic:

- Google Sheets CRM plus follow-up automation for solo consultants and freelancers.

### Reporter's Notebook

The Reporter's Notebook covers public research logs, source trails, public-record methods, FOIA/public-archive notes, and investigation tooling. It is public and visible, but it is not a raw reporting dump.

Publish only cleaned notes with clear sourcing, no sensitive leads, no private account material, and no unresolved allegations framed as fact.

## Editorial Boundaries

- Raw reporting work stays private in the writing workspace until it is cleaned for publication.
- Public-record and FOIA work should favor public-by-default publication when publishable.
- Sensitive, source-protective, legally delicate, embargoed, or rough exploratory work stays out of the site.
- Automation content should prioritize working artifacts over generic commentary.
- AI-assisted work should be disclosed by method when it materially affects the artifact or analysis.

## First 30 Days

1. Reposition the homepage, About page, navigation, metadata, and blog labels around the public lab and notebook identity.
2. Add the Reporter's Notebook route and filter notebook posts there.
3. Publish one Automation Lab anchor post with a Google Sheets CRM/follow-up checklist and starter script.
4. Publish one Reporter's Notebook anchor post that explains the research method and boundaries.
5. Track whether the automation anchor produces useful follow-up: service inquiries, template requests, newsletter/subscriber interest, or reusable implementation patterns.

## Monetization Paths

- Productized automation setup for solo operators and tiny teams.
- Paid templates and implementation checklists after enough repeated demand appears.
- Affiliate links for tools Josh actually uses in implemented workflows.
- Consulting or retainers for clients who need custom workflow systems.
- Newsletter sponsorships only after the audience has a clear identity and durable open rate.

## Non-Goals

- No mass-generated SEO site network in this pass.
- No separate domain until a niche thesis has evidence.
- No YMYL content, thin affiliate reviews, or content written mainly for search engines.
- No paid external API runs or account-touching publication workflows in v1.
